Eddie Guo (he/him)

For CMSA President, Class of 2025


Hello friends and colleagues! My name is Eddie, and I am running to be your CMSA President. You may know me as the person who shares announcements and study resources with you all, or perhaps as the “Discord guy.” I want to begin by saying that you all have extraordinary backgrounds and stories; it would be a privilege to serve you in a formal capacity.

A little about me: I have served in student government and on national committees dedicated to education and student advocacy throughout the past few years. During this time, I collaborated with University of Alberta faculty and students to redesign my program, Engineering Physics, with new courses and policies to better serve student and industry needs. My goal, if elected, is to continue serving and ensuring the best possible medical school experience for each of you.

My Vision for the CMSA

I envision three overarching themes for our class and the CMSA:


  • Creating a community where every student's voice is heard,
  • Fostering a culture of collaboration and support amongst our class, and
  • Facilitating a medical school experience with ample opportunities and resources to succeed.

Here is how I aim to achieve that vision.


Vision Action Item
To create a community where every student's voice is heard, I will
  1. Advocate, represent, and act on your concerns and suggestions with faculty, partners, and the CMSA to improve your medical school experience,
  2. Create an easily accessible platform to anonymously submit your concerns and suggestions, and
  3. Compile and share an annual evaluation report outlining CMSA outcomes driven by your inputs to ensure accountability, transparency, and efficacy.
To foster a culture of collaboration and support amongst our class, I will
  1. Create an academic support and resource platform where our peers can share and access study materials (e.g., the Google Drive, Discord),
  2. Share accessible mental health resources and fix the currently broken mistreatment reporting link on Osler,
  3. Allocate a greater portion of the CMSA budget towards student wellness items (e.g., a platform to easily access support systems, more functional microwaves in the student lounge, better pool tables),
  4. Develop a tool to keep track of relevant student conversations (e.g., study groups, post-exam party groups), and
  5. Continue to curate and share tailored study resources, career planning tools, budgeting tools, Anki decks, and much, much more.
To facilitate a medical school experience with ample opportunities and resources to succeed, I will
  1. Develop career-planning resources (e.g., guide to CaRMS, specialty selection resources, guides to shadowing, alternative pathways after medical school),
  2. Collaborate with faculty and CMSA partners to increase funding to support students facing hardships (e.g., financial difficulties, late acceptance off the waitlist, student-led initiatives),
  3. Ensure efficient operation of the CMSA to promote and implement student-centric initiatives,
  4. Advocate for the improvement of pain points in our medical school experience (e.g., faster return of exam grades), and
  5. Share relevant announcements of essential items and UME policies and procedures in a student-accessible and easily digestible manner.

My personal promise to you is that I will (i) continue working my hardest to serve you and (ii) tailor CMSA initiatives with your input to optimize your medical school experience. Further, I promise to serve your interests and represent you faithfully in all CMSA initiatives and projects.

Tl;dr

I envision a supportive and collegial community. I will achieve that vision through accessible support and resources, financial allocation to student-centric projects, improvement of pain points (e.g., faster return of exam grades), and collaboration with you to create the best possible medical school experience.


If you read to this point, thank you for sticking around! It would be the utmost privilege to serve each and every one of you.
